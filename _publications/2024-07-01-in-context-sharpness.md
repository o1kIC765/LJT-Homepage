This work introduces in-context sharpness as a signal for detecting hallucinations in large language model outputs. From an inner representation perspective, we show that the sharpness of token representations correlates with the likelihood of hallucinated content, providing an effective mitigation mechanism.

Co-authors: Shiqi Chen, Miao Xiong, Zhengxuan Wu, Teng Xiao, Siyang Gao, Junxian He.
